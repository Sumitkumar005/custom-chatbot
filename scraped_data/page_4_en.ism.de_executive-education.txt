# Business Informatics Courses

At ISM, students are trained to bridge the gap between information systems and business management, making them highly valued for their communication and interface skills. Our business information courses offer you a valuable education that perfectly combines theory and practice.

Get to know us and find out more about the opportunities to study Business Informatics at one of Germany's leading business schools. You will not only gain in-depth knowledge in the core areas of business and information systems, but also valuable insights through our global networks and partnerships.

## Why study business informatics?

You learn to ensure **digital transformation** with **suitable information** and **communication systems** and to turn data into valuable knowledge. Companies need experts in the fields of business informatics, data management, business intelligence and data science. Whether it's about efficiently optimizing production, gaining knowledge from data sets or launching smart products on the market. As a business information scientist, you ensure that information technology provides valuable insights for the company and further develops digital business models. So if you want to **apply IT and digital to the problems of tomorrow** then business informatics is exactly the right program for you.

The study program in Germany combines a solid grounding in computer programming, complex information systems, mathematics, and statistics with business management. On the labour market Competences in Business informatics, which combine the requirements of business administration and information technology, are in demand more than ever.

## Business informatics courses at ISM

[#### Bachelor of Information Systems

- **Degree:** Bachelor of Science
- **Standard period of study:** 6 semester (7 semester with the Global Track)
- **ECTS credits:** 180 ECTS (6 semster)/210 ECTS (7 semester)
- **Language requirements:** English
- **Campuses:** Dortmund, Munich or Hamburg
- **Program start:** March (spring term) and September (fall term)

How can data be organized and used for innovation? In the information systems bachelor's degree program, you learn to combine knowledge from business administration with current trends in business informatics.

Modules such as **Web Engineering, Computer Science, Programming** and **Business Information Systems** are integrated with modules such as **Business Administration, Marketing and Economics**. Programming courses and hacking seminars to uncover security gaps, prepare you for the interfaces between IT and management in the company.

Learn more](/full-degree-students/bachelor-programs/bachelor-information-systems/overview)

[#### Master of Business Intelligence & Data Science

- **Degree:** Master of Science
- **Standard period of study:** 3 semester plus 1 semester thesis
- **ECTS credits:** 180 ECTS (6 semster)/210 ECTS (7 semester
- **Language requirements:** English
- **Campuses:** Dortmund, Munich or Hamburg
- **Program start:** September (fall term)

This Business Informatics Master’s program in Germany equips you with essential skills to manage and analyze data effectively—enabling smarter business decisions in an increasingly digital world. You will learn how to extract insights through efficient data queries, process automation, and intelligent data analysis. Key questions you'll explore include:

- How can data be collected, managed, and analyzed across business units?
- What data processing methods (e.g., SQL, data warehousing, ETL) are most effective?
- How can digital systems support business planning, performance monitoring, and model-driven management?

Throughout your studies, you’ll develop expertise in: Data Science and Business Analytics, Optimization and Forecasting Techniques, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML), Enterprise Information Systems, IT Strategy and Digital Transformation.

You will gain the ability to simplify complex problems and identify actionable insights using real-world datasets. By identifying trends and patterns, you'll support strategic decision-making and drive innovation in business environments.

Learn more](/full-degree-students/master-programs/master-business-intelligence/overview)

## Why study business informatics in Germany

Benefit from a high-quality educational system to help you advance in your career. At the private university in Germany, you do not need to fulfill an NC (numerus clausus). We get to know you and your motivation and skills in an admission process. The program is 100% in English and it is a face-to-face program on campus. The small learning groups allow direct exchange with professors and fellow students as well as practical partners from the business world. Insights into companies and lectures by CEOs as well as an integrated semester abroad broaden your experience.

For more tips and information on financing your studies and visas, please [contact your student advisor](/services/study-advisor).

#### What do you need to study business information systems?

You do not have to be a math genius to study business informatics. You should have a good basic understanding of mathematics and you do not have to be able to program right at the beginning of your studies. You will learn it during your studies. Organizational and communication skills are other good prerequisites.

## Your career opportunities with a business informatics degree

In the area of business informatics, versatile occupational fields are emerging in the global economy in almost all industries in which IT systems and data are used. By combining the necessary application knowledge with the business consulting skills, business informatics specialists and graduates are becoming important drivers of change and data analysis projects.

Your expertise is in demand in independent data-driven problem solutions in international consulting projects. You are the **interface between business and IT experts** and independently solve **data-driven problems in international consulting projects**. After graduating in business informatics, you will have the opportunity to work as a **business informatics specialist, developer, product manager, IT-Consultant or in marketing**, among other positions.

Moreover, according to a [*Forbes article*](https://www.forbes.com/sites/carolinecastrillon/2025/04/21/10-high-paying-jobs-that-help-you-avoid-burnout-in-2025/ "Data Scientist among the 10 High paying jobs that help avoid burnout"), Data Scientist is one of the best-paying careers that also **offers good work-life balance and helps avoid burnout**. The role mostly involves working independently with flexible schedules. The work is analytical, which means there are clear objectives and measurable outcomes, so there's less ambiguity, which can lead to stress. This makes it not only a smart academic choice, but also a wise lifestyle decision.

If you have a good understanding of statistics, mathematics, are eager to understand machine learning, and have an analytical mind, then a degree program in this field might be the right one for you!

#### What jobs and functions do graduates perform?

- Data Consultant
- Big Data Engineering
- Data Scientist & Data Analyst/Chief Data Officer
- Project manager/Chief Information Office
- System architects in Management/System consultant
- Forecasting Consultants
- Machine Learning Engineers
- Process optimizer
- Business Information Scientist
- E-Business Manager/E-Commerce specialist
- IT-Management (IT-Consultants/IT-Strategy IT-coordinator)

#### Typical job tasks and activities

- Analyze, optimize and implement information and communication systems
- Analyze IT requirements taking customer needs
- Communicate with technical and non-technical professionals
- Present technical information in a way that is also understandable for non-technical personnel
- Evaluate and develop application software
- Holistic project management in the implementation of IT-heavy projects
- Identify, model and optimize business processes and workflows
- Developing innovations from data (Smart Data)
- Develop strategies and concepts for a data-driven corporate culture
